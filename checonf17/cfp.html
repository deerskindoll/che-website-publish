Call for Speakers

CheConf 2017 Topic Areas

CheConf brings together the Eclipse Che community, its user and surrounding ecosystem for a day long virtual conference covering topics that affect cloud development and containerization. This year the focus is on using Che in teams and extending Che drive growth in developer communities.

  • Eclipse Che use cases for teams.
  • Extending and customizing Eclipse Che.
  • Eclipse Che as part of a developer team workflow.
  • LSP-compliant language servers.
  • Keycloak and user authentication.

Session Types

There are three types of sessions at CheConf:

  • Mini-talks: 15 minutes.
  • Standard talks: 45 minutes.
  • Tutorials: 75 minutes.

Mini-Talks

Short talks that quickly highlight an area of Eclipse Che or of the ecosystem, especially tips and tricks. Alternatively a quick demo of a Che-built solution is also appreciated. Regardless, due to their short time mini-talks should be all nutrition with no filler!

Standard Talks

For standard talks we are looking for sessions that will outline successful use cases with best practices and technical details, highlight new technology areas and provide an introduction into how to get the most from them, or discuss the details of related technologies in the cloud development ecosystem.

Tutorials

Tutorials are the fun part of any CheConf - they're where developers show each other how to build something with or for Che. Tutorials walk through writing, building and deploying the code necessary to explain the technology.

Submitting a Session

The key to getting a session accepted is the abstract - ensure that it's clear who your audience should be, why they should attend and what you'll cover. To submit a session please send a Twitter DM to: Brad Micklea (@bradmicklea).

  • Your name and email address.
  • Session type (mini-talk, standard talk or tutorial).
  • Topic which you are focused on.
  • An abstract of the session (maximum 250 words), this will be used to advertise the session - it's your chance to really "sell" the value of attending your session.
  • Optional: link to the slides you'll present.
List your target personas and/or industries - who is the audience for your story? What is the take-away or action item the attendee walks away with? Once you have those 5 things outlined you are ready to apply to speak 6. Once on the submissions page, be as precise and descriptive as possible - the more direct you are the easier it is for our content teams to understand exactly what you would like to speak about 7. Let us know what other speaking experience that you have 8. Think about if you would prefer to speak in a 20 or 40 minute session.
