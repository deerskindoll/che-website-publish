Call for Speakers

CheConf 2017 Topic Areas

CheConf brings together the Eclipse Che community, its user and surrounding ecosystem for a day long virtual conference covering topics that affect cloud development and containerization. This year the focus is on using Che in teams and extending Che drive growth in developer communities.

  • Eclipse Che case studies: how and why you use Che in your team or organization, or what you have done to base your company's product on Che.
  • Extending Eclipse Che: building plugins, integrations or white labelling.
  • LSP-compliant language servers: how to build, how to integrate or what's next for language servers.
  • Cloud development: Che-related technologies that are relevant to cloud-native development, these could include process or tool discussions.

Session Types

There are three types of sessions at CheConf:

  • Talks: 30 minutes.
  • Demos: 15 minutes.
  • Tutorials: 60 minutes.

Talks

Talks should focus on one of the main topic areas listed above. Content should be practical with minimal "marketing." Focusing on specific use cases with technical details and examples of how goals were achieved or challenges overcome will bring your submission to the top of the list.

Demos

In some cases it's best to let the product speak for itself so we'd love to see a quick demo (some context-setting slides are fine) that shows how you've used Che to achieve a goal. Due to their short time demos should be all nutrition with no filler!

Tutorials

Tutorials walk through writing, building and deploying the code necessary to explain the technology. With only an hour consider the scope of what you're presenting so that you can fit everything in.

Submitting a Session

The key to getting a session accepted is the abstract - ensure that it's clear on who your audience is, why they should attend and what you'll cover.

  • Your name and email address.
  • Session type (talk, demo or tutorial).
  • Topic which you are focused on.
  • An abstract of the session (maximum 250 words), this will be used to advertise the session - it's your chance to really "sell" the value of attending your session.
  • Optional: link to the slides you'll present.
