Call for Speakers

CheConf brings together the Eclipse Che community, its user and surrounding ecosystem for a day long virtual conference covering topics that affect cloud development and containerization. This year the focus is on using Che in teams and extending Che drive growth in developer communities.

Submit a Session Proposal

Submitting a Session

The key to getting a session accepted is the abstract - ensure that it's clear who your audience should be, why they should attend and what you'll cover. The abstract should be a maximum of 250 words and advertise your session - it's your chance to really "sell" the value of attending your session. You can even include links to slides you'll present if you have them.

Align your proposal to one of the CheConf 2018 topic areas:

  • Eclipse Che use cases for teams.
  • Extending and customizing Eclipse Che.
  • Eclipse Che as part of a developer team workflow.
  • LSP-compliant language servers.
  • Keycloak and user authentication.

Session Types

There are three types of sessions at CheConf:

  • Mini-talks (15 minutes): Short talks that quickly highlight an area of Eclipse Che or of the ecosystem, especially tips and tricks. Alternatively a quick demo of a Che-built solution is also appreciated. Regardless, due to their short time mini-talks should be all nutrition with no filler!
  • Standard talks (45 minutes): For standard talks we are looking for sessions that will outline successful use cases with best practices and technical details, highlight new technology areas and provide an introduction into how to get the most from them, or discuss the details of related technologies in the cloud development ecosystem.
  • Tutorials (75 minutes): Tutorials are the fun part of any CheConf - they're where developers show each other how to build something with or for Che. Tutorials walk through writing, building and deploying the code necessary to explain the technology.
