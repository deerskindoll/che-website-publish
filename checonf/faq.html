FAQ

What is CheConf?

CheConf is the Eclipse Che users’ conference. This will be the first year of the event and it will be held on Tuesday, November 15th, 2016. This year, CheConf will be a virtual conference. With hundreds of thousands of Eclipse Che users around the world, we wanted to make sure that anyone, anywhere can experience this event.

How does a virtual user conference work?

CheConf will work much like a regular conference. We have a set agenda and speakers, with sessions that you can attend from your computer. A conference agenda with links to each session will be sent out days before the event. On the day of the conference, you simply click on the session you want to attend, and you will be connected. There will be an opportunity for you to live chat and ask questions with other virtual conference attendees. You will also be able to live chat with the Che team.

How many sessions can I attend?

As many as you like. We want you to experience CheConf your way. The first keynote session begins at 9:00AM EDT, with the last session ending at 3:00PM EDT. You can attend as many sessions as you like. You can stay connected all day, or you can pop in and out of the sessions you want to attend. View Sessions.

How do I register?

Registration is free and takes seconds. Once you register, you will receive updates and details on how to access sessions, participate in live chat, and where to go after the event for post-conference resources. Register Today.

How can I get involved?

Eclipse Che is an open source project. Open source projects need the power of the community to succeed. Being a Che user and advocate, and sharing experiences with your peers is the best way to help keep Che growing. We love when people write blogs, tweet and make contribution to the Che project.
