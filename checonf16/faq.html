FAQ

What is CheConf?

CheConf is the Eclipse Che users’ conference. 2016 was the first year of the event and it was held on Tuesday, November 15th, 2016. CheConf 2016 was a virtual conference: with hundreds of thousands of Eclipse Che users around the world, we wanted to make sure that anyone, anywhere can experience this event. The sessions can be viewed on the sessions page.

How does a virtual user conference work?

CheConf will work much like a regular conference. We have a set agenda and speakers, with sessions that you can attend from your computer. A conference agenda with links to each session will be sent out days before the event. On the day of the conference, you simply click on the session you want to attend, and you will be connected. There will be an opportunity for you to live chat and ask questions with other virtual conference attendees. You will also be able to live chat with the Che team.

How many sessions can I attend?

As many as you like. We want you to experience CheConf your way. The first keynote session begins at 11:00AM EST. You can attend as many sessions as you like. You can stay connected all day, or you can pop in and out of the sessions you want to attend. View Sessions.

How can I get involved?

Eclipse Che is an open source project. Open source projects need the power of the community to succeed. Being a Che user and advocate, and sharing experiences with your peers is the best way to help keep Che growing. We love when people write blogs, tweet and make contribution to the Che project.
